Helping Rosebud users see the patterns in their own writing.

A 3-day design trial. Scope, proposed solution, effort estimates, and how we’d measure whether it worked.

Problem

Journaling tools stop at the entry: notebook in, nothing out. Users rarely re-read, rarely spot their own patterns, rarely feel the accumulation of months of writing. The challenge is turning a pile of text into something a user can see themselves in, without making the writing feel surveilled in the moment.

Proposed solution

Rosebud becomes an anthology of your own writing. History stays archival and unmonitored. Patterns is a separate destination, story cards, behavioral highlights, people, echoes, and a third-person “Story of You” read in audiobook format, bridged back to History by quoted fragments from real entries. Onboarding asks one goal-oriented question and routes the answer to loggers and patterns behind the scenes, so users never learn the app’s taxonomy.

Scope & effort

Item Effort
Anthology Results surface (week + quarter view) Story cards, emotional arc, people donut, echoes, Story of You. High
Pattern tracking onboarding (intent-first) One goal picker, opinionated weekly summary, Apple Health sync offered on the confirmation screen. Low
History archive + Bookmarks Chronological list and entry detail sheet. No analysis overlay. Low
Post-entry tracking chip/sheet Lightweight tagging after Finish Entry. Low
Real Apple Health integration Out of trial scope. Prototype shows the sync option in the UI but doesn’t pull real data. High

High-level requirements

  • History is unwatched. No inline analysis on raw entries.
  • Patterns is editorial, not clinical. Every insight is grounded in quoted fragments from real entries.
  • Hide the taxonomy. Users never see “logger” or “pattern” during setup. The split happens in the backend.
  • Opinionated defaults. Weekly summary is a stated fact, not a user decision.
  • Timeframe-aware results. Week view and 3-month view are different reads, not the same view with different numbers.

Success metrics & how we’d test

KPI Target How we’d test
Onboarding completion > 70% Funnel instrumentation, A/B vs current flow, 4-week cohort.
Day-7 Patterns visit rate > 40% Tab-open events, segmented by onboarding version.
Weekly summary open rate > 50% Notification + in-app reads, benchmarked against email norms.
Pattern save/share per week > 1 / user CTA events on bookmark and share. Proxy for emotional resonance.
4-week retention lift + 15% Cohort retention A/B, plus 5–8 qualitative user interviews.
